# Research Plan: CBT vs DBT for Treatment-Resistant Depression

**Query:** Compare effectiveness of CBT vs DBT for treatment-resistant depression, focusing on remission rates, dropout rates, and long-term outcomes

**Purpose:** Comparative survey — synthesize existing RCT and cohort evidence to inform clinical decision-making

---

## Research Areas

### 1. Background: Treatment-Resistant Depression (TRD)
- Define TRD (typically failure of ≥2 adequate antidepressant trials)
- Prevalence estimates and clinical burden
- Current first-line pharmacological and psychotherapeutic approaches
- Why CBT and DBT are candidates for TRD populations

### 2. Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) — Mechanisms and Evidence Base
- Core mechanisms: cognitive restructuring, behavioural activation, thought records
- Evidence in non-resistant depression (benchmark)
- RCT evidence in TRD specifically
- Effect sizes for remission, response, and symptom reduction

### 3. Dialectical Behaviour Therapy (DBT) — Mechanisms and Evidence Base
- Origin in borderline personality disorder; adaptation for unipolar depression
- Core skills: mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, interpersonal effectiveness
- Evidence in TRD and recurrent MDD
- Effect sizes for remission and symptom reduction

### 4. Head-to-Head Comparisons
- Direct RCTs or comparative cohort studies
- Remission rate differences (primary outcome)
- Response rate and symptom score differences (secondary)
- Moderators: baseline severity, comorbid personality pathology, prior treatment history

### 5. Dropout and Adherence
- Dropout rates across CBT and DBT arms
- Reasons for dropout (burden of homework, session length, skill demands)
- Predictors of non-completion

### 6. Long-Term Outcomes and Follow-Up
- Relapse and recurrence rates at 6-month, 1-year, and 2-year follow-up
- Durability of remission
- Booster sessions and maintenance strategies

### 7. Limitations and Research Gaps
- Sample sizes and generalisability
- Heterogeneity of TRD definitions across studies
- Lack of active control conditions
- Need for precision medicine approaches

---

## Search Strategy
- Databases: PubMed, Google Scholar, PsycINFO
- Key terms: "CBT treatment-resistant depression", "DBT treatment-resistant depression", "cognitive behavioural therapy refractory depression RCT", "dialectical behaviour therapy depression remission"
- Date range: 2015–2024
- Include: RCTs, meta-analyses, systematic reviews, large cohort studies
- Exclude: case reports, non-peer-reviewed sources

---

## Deliverable
A structured comparative report (~1000 words) with a summary table of study characteristics, remission rates, dropout rates, and follow-up durations.
